I am a PhD candidate at the [School of Political Science, University of Haifa](https://poli.haifa.ac.il/en), working within the Canetti Lab under the supervision of Prof. Daphna Canetti and Prof. Itzik Ben Israel (Tel Aviv University). I am based in Berlin, Germany.

My research sits at the intersection of **political psychology, AI-mediated conflict, and disinformation**. My dissertation examines whether exposure to AI-generated content — including deepfake videos and bot-driven narratives — shapes public support for state surveillance across different political and cultural contexts. I use cross-national survey experiments spanning Israel, India, and the United States.

Before entering academia, I worked as an investigative journalist at **WION** and **The Statesman**, and as a disinformation consultant. I hold a B.Tech in Bioinformatics, which informs my quantitative and computational approach to social science research.

My broader research interests include:
- Terrorism and political attitudes
- AI-generated media and psychological effects
- Bot-driven emotional contagion
- Cyber conflict and information warfare
- Public opinion and surveillance

I am currently based at the intersection of academic research and policy relevance, with active engagement in Berlin's research and policy community.
